MOSAIC PHOTO & VIDEO FILTER

A procedurally-generated video filter using Processing (Java) that creates a mosaic with a predetermined array of images. I've applied the filter to the viral video of internet sweetheart Keke Palmer for Vanity Fair's 'lie detector' series.

RACE, REPARATIONS & REMEMBRANCE: THE LASTING IMPACT OF ANTIBLACKNESS AND SYSTEMIC RACISM IN MODERN GERMAN SOCIETY

An independent study charting antiblack racism in German social hierarchy from pre-unification to present day (circa 2021). This research was undertaken with the support of an Undergraduate Research Award and later presented virtually.

REJECT REGULAR

A highly experiential campaign developed for the 2022 National Student Advertising Competition client, Meta, positioning the Quest 2 headset as "college essential" - the gateway to keeping 18-24 year olds infinitely entertained and socially connected. Our research, insights and creative activations as well as an invigorating final presentation earned us 5th place.

GIVE A SH*T TOOLKIT

A social media campaign developed in collaboration with the University of Kansas Business School to support the "Protect KU" plan to make the transition back to in-person instruction safer for all members of the community.

KANSAS AFRICAN STUDIES CENTER WEBSITE MIGRATION

To help the center comply with University regulations, I worked to migrate the website from a CMS we were pivoting from and helped redesign the website using Drupal, keeping the interface consistent with the institutions's color scehemes, approved templates, etc. Accessibility was heavily emphasized in the migration, and i was responsible for ensuring all content and file formats met the expectations.

I WAS A STRANGER AND YOU DISREGARDED ME

Instead of writing an essay for my 'Diversity in Media' class, I created a fictional podcast predicated on breaking down complex issues to provide more context and improve the media literacy skills of my listeners. Strap in! For my pilot episode we will be discussing the portrayal of immigrants in the media.

FROM THE TOP (2021)

What do Kansans know about hip-hop? Quite a bit as it turns out. Hip-hop chorepgrapher and professor at the University of Kansas, Maya Tillman-Rayton, takes us through her journey as a dancer and practitioner while preparing for the 2021 University Dance Company Showcase: Bodies of Text. Filmed, produced and edited by me for the virtual exhibition: Pass Me The Mic.

QUEERING THE DIGITAL ARCHIVE

For my master's thesis, I combined technical and theoretical analysis to make the case for newer forms and approaches to digital archivism in order to protect queer naratives while also restoring agency to the most marginalized within the LGBTQ+ umbrella. For my research, I investigated popular web 2.0 and 3.0 platforms and technologies, higlighting their affordances and shortcomings, ultimately providing a foundation for future research.

THE LONDON REVIEW (2022 EDITION)

I contributed to the University of Kansas Honors annual publication of The London Review, where students elect to study abroad for a cultural immersion and then collectively produce the magazine, filled with essays, stories and artwork reflecting on the experience.
